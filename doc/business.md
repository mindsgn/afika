## Pocket Money – Business Notes

### Target market (South Africa)

- Everyday South Africans who need to send and receive money quickly and cheaply, especially:
  - Family remittances (within SA and to neighbours).
  - Side-hustle income and gig workers getting paid.
  - Small merchants who want faster “settlement” than traditional EFTs.
- Early adopters who are comfortable with mobile apps and basic crypto concepts.

### Product thesis

- Use USDC on Ethereum (initially Sepolia for testing, then mainnet or L2 later) as a digital rand proxy:
  - Stable value relative to USD.
  - Programmable and globally interoperable.
- Hide blockchain complexity:
  - Users think in RAND and USD, not gas and gwei.
  - Email-based sending, address book, and simple “cash app style” UI.

### Monetization ideas

- **FX spread and conversion fees**
  - Charge a small spread when converting between USDC and ZAR via manual bank deposits/withdrawals.
- **Withdrawal / payout fees**
  - Flat or percentage-based fee when users cash out to a South African bank account.
- **Premium features**
  - Priority support, higher limits, advanced analytics for power users and merchants.
- **B2B APIs**
  - Offer payouts-as-a-service for local fintechs or platforms that want to send USDC to their users.

### Medium-term roadmap (post-MVP)

- Move from testnet to production networks (L2s with cheaper fees).
- Add card / virtual card integration through banking-as-a-service partners.
- Reintroduce smart accounts and paymasters to sponsor gas for mainstream users.
- Add compliance tooling (KYC/AML workflows) and reporting.
